The Problem Worth Solving

Every teacher has had that student.

Arms crossed. Eyes down. Present in body, absent in spirit. We call them disengaged. We call them unmotivated. Sometimes we quietly write them off.

But what if we're wrong?

The truth is: adolescents are never truly disengaged. They are deeply, fiercely engaged — in music, in relationships, in the world outside your classroom door. The question isn't whether they can engage. It's whether we've given them a reason to.

Dr. Witherspoon's work is built around one shift: stop asking "How do I get them to care?" and start asking "How do I make this worth caring about?"

The Framework

The MATTER ModelThe framework at the heart of every Bring Learning to Life session

Six moves any teacher can use — in any subject — to connect content to the real lives of adolescents. This is the original framework at the center of every Dr. Witherspoon session.

M
Make It Explicit

Don't assume students see why it matters. Tell them — in their language, before the lesson starts.

A
Anchor to Their World

Pull from culture, community, or current events they recognize. Meet them where they already are.

T
Tie to a Real Problem

Not hypothetical — actual. An unresolved question or issue students could do something about.

T
Trust Their Expertise

Students are experts on their own lives. Ask before you assume what matters to them.

E
Elevate Student Voice

Let students shape how content shows up. Voice and choice unlock intrinsic motivation.

R
Repeat the Culture

Relevance isn't a one-time move. It's a classroom culture — built lesson by lesson, day by day.

About

Dr. Ivetha Witherspoon

Dr. Ivetha Witherspoon is a nationally recognized educator, literacy expert, and professional development facilitator whose sessions are known for being as engaging to experience as they are practical to apply.

With deep expertise in adolescent literacy, content-area writing, vocabulary instruction, and student engagement, Dr. Witherspoon has delivered transformative professional development to grades 4–12 educators across more than a dozen states — from California to Massachusetts, Louisiana to Ohio.

"She doesn't just talk about engagement. She models it — every technique, every strategy, every time."

Described by participants as "the best presenter I have ever experienced in my education and career," Dr. Witherspoon brings infectious energy, genuine warmth, and a no-excuses belief that every student can be reached — when every teacher is equipped to reach them.

Her work is grounded in a simple but powerful premise: engagement is not a student trait. It is a teaching outcome. When educators are given the right framework and the right strategies, they don't just teach better — they transform the experience of school for the adolescents who need it most.

Dr. Witherspoon is available for keynotes, workshops, full-day professional development, and multi-session training series — in person and virtually.
